CAIPICAJU

Perfect drink for chill on the sun, in any brasilian beach or anywere else!

The Caipirinha is Brazil’s national cocktail; made with cachaça, sugar and lime and sometimes with other local fruits. Cachaça, also known as pinga, caninha, and a multitude of colloquial names, is Brazil’s most common distilled alcoholic beverage.

The caju is the fruit of the cashew tree and is a kidney or boxing-glove shaped drupe that grows at the end of the cashew apple. The drupe develops first on the tree, and then the pedicel expands to become the cashew apple. Within the fruit is a single seed which is often considered a nut in the culinary sense.

INGREDIENTS
  • 1 cashew fruit (caju), sliced
  • 1 lime, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 5 oz of cachaçha
  • 1/2 cup of ice
  • 3 coriander seeds
STEPS
  1. Fill shaker with cubed or crushed ice, squeeze limes and cashew fruit into the glass
  2. Add sugar, coriader seeds and crush with pestle
  3. Add cachaça, shake and serve
